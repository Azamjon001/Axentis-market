The Problem

Commerce is fragmented and manual.

04 / 22

Today a seller stitches together a chat storefront, a paper ledger, a separate POS, a courier's phone number and a spreadsheet. Nothing talks to anything. Customers feel it too.

Tool sprawl

Sellers juggle 5–6 disconnected apps for sales, stock, delivery and money. Data never reconciles.

Paper accounting

Profit, debts and expenses live in notebooks. Owners rarely know their true margin.

Blind inventory

No live stock counts means overselling, dead stock and missed reorders.

Chaotic delivery

Orders coordinated by phone. No tracking, no courier accountability, no proof of delivery.

Hard to be found

Small sellers have no discovery channel and no way to reach nearby customers.

No growth engine

Discounts, promos and loyalty are ad-hoc. There's no data to market intelligently.

Security & Compliance

Trust engineered into every layer.

16 / 22
Authentication

Phone + password or SMS one-time codes. Codes are hashed (HMAC-SHA256), expire in 5 min, capped attempts & sends.

Authorization

JWT middleware attaches role, company and phone to every request. Scoped access; PIN-locked panel sections.

Encryption

TLS in transit via Nginx; passwords hashed with bcrypt; secrets kept out of source, injected at runtime.

Backups

A single Postgres source of truth with routine backups and migration-driven, reproducible schema.

Audit Logs

Sensitive admin and finance actions are traceable; a dedicated security center surfaces platform activity.

Fraud Detection

Rate-limited auth per IP, abuse thresholds on OTP, and complaint/moderation workflows for bad actors.
